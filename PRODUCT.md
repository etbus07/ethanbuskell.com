# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, one surface:

- **University admissions tutors** (UK engineering departments) skim-reading dozens of applicants. They arrive sceptical, on a laptop, with seconds of attention. They want fast evidence of capability and seriousness, not personality theatre.
- **Employers / internship leads** evaluating a young engineer for real work. They look for shipped projects, range (software + hardware + CAD), and signal that the person finishes things.

Secondary: Ethan himself, who treats the site as a living CV he updates as projects ship. The job-to-be-done for every visitor is the same — *decide, quickly, whether this person is capable and precise.*

## Product Purpose

A single-scrolling portfolio that functions as a living CV for Ethan B., a UK sixth-form engineering applicant (predicted A* in Further Maths, Maths, Computer Science, Physics). It exists to demonstrate capability and precision through real work — web platforms, hardware, and Fusion 360 CAD — rather than to assert them in prose. Success = a tutor or employer leaves convinced the work is real and the person is rigorous, and has the CV/contact path one click away.

## Brand Personality

**Precise · engineered · understated.** The voice is a calm, competent engineer: states facts, shows the work, never oversells. Confidence is communicated through restraint and accuracy (correct technical conventions, real specs, tight typography), not through volume. Closer to a well-made instrument than a hype deck.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dark-mode developer portfolio** — near-black + neon accent, identical icon-card grid, "I build cool stuff" energy. The default this brief must not become.
- **Flashy SaaS / startup landing** — hero-metric template, gradient-on-everything, glass cards, big rounded corners, motion for its own sake.
- **Cluttered CV / résumé template** — every field visible at once, no hierarchy, a document dump rather than a designed page.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, don't describe it.** Real projects, real CAD, real specs do the persuading. Cut adjectives.
2. **Precision is the message.** Technical-drawing conventions, accurate units, exact alignment — the medium proves the claim.
3. **Restraint signals confidence.** One accent, generous space, no decoration that isn't load-bearing. Loud is insecure.
4. **Every element earns its pixel.** If it doesn't add evidence or aid navigation, it's removed (Nielsen #8).
5. **Engineering identity, not costume.** The aesthetic should be unmistakably *this* engineer's — derived from hardware/CAD/maths — never a theme bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Concretely: ≥4.5:1 contrast for body text (≥3:1 large), full keyboard navigation with visible focus states, a skip-to-content link past the fixed nav, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all entrance/scroll animations, labelled controls (no icon/glyph-only buttons without accessible names), and no meaning carried by colour alone.
